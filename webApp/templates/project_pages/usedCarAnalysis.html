Exploratory Data Analysis

Over all the price distribution quite normal. As one would expect the distribution mirrors the new car market with a skew towards the cheaper vehicles. It appears that the average car price hovers around $6000, with the median around $15000.

Something that may stand out is the spike in car prices around the zero dollar mark. This is due to the popular trick of listing your car at $0 which stems from people ordering the listings from least expensive to most expensive. You can see a similar effect in a phonebook with companies names starting with "a", "aa", or even "aaa".

Nothing appears to be out of the ordinary for this distribution. It appears that most people will sell their car after 5 to 8 years of use. It is also worth noting that there is a large dip in available cars for sale from 2009 and 2010. This suggests that less cars were purchased during this period due to economical hardships.

Here we see average used car on craigslist has around 100,000 miles on the odometer. We also see that it is popular to list the car around 0 miles on the odometer, a similar trick to listing the car for $0. The fact that this distribution is almost the average brings up an interesting question of "Where do all the old cars go after they hit 100k miles".

We have always heard that "American Made" was a big selling point for cars but this really shows the magnitude of that preference. With Ford, and Chevrolet dominating the marking it is interesting to see GMC and Ram a much smaller part of the used market.
