As a quick weekend project I spent time learning BeautifulSoup, a popular python library for extracting data from HTML and XML files. As my Mother was looking at starting a Tea company I figured I would make the learning experience useful for at least her. To do this I pulled data from one of the largest tea distributors in the USA, Dethlefsen & Balk. From their website I pulled the following data:

  • Product Number (string)
  • Product's Name (string)
  • Product Description (string)
  • Quantity per Container (string)
  • Min and Max Brew Times (int)
  • Min and Max Brew Temperatures (int)
  • Serving Size (int)
  • Organic Status (boolean)

Aggregated data was then formatted and outputted as a excel spreadsheet for easy of access.

The project provided a great way to learn about BeautifulSoup and some good practice. While not the most beautiful of code, my repository provides a good reference on how to use BeautifulSoup.

Note to Reader: After collecting data on tea and copious amounts of tea samples I have realized the vast majority of "Independent" Tea stores sell repackaged tea from Dethlefsen & Balk. You'r favorite expensive tea store is most likely selling the same tea as the tea store across town; or better yet you might just find it online.
